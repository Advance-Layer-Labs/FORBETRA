1. First touch — anyone who lands on the URL

The landing page is intentionally narrow: it assumes the visitor was either invited by a coach or already knows what Forbetra is. The two CTAs ("Sign in" warm gradient, "Create account" secondary) are the entire pitch.

Q for you: If/when you start organic marketing, this page needs a "what is this?" hero. For coach-invited users it's fine as-is.

Sign in / sign up

Both are hosted by Clerk. The current sign-in form supports Google + LinkedIn SSO + email/password. Sign-up adds a Username field (per the improvement plan, that should probably be killed — uncommon for coaching apps).

The legal surface — usually invisible

Privacy, SMS Terms, SMS Consent, and Terms of Service. These pages exist primarily to satisfy compliance requirements (especially the A2P 10DLC campaign for SMS), not as primary user destinations. But the SMS Consent page in particular is a high-leverage compliance surface — if it goes down, SMS breaks.

2. The Individual journey — your client's 12 weeks

This is the single most important persona to design well. They visit ~1–2× per week for 12 weeks. The UX that gets them through week 1 determines whether they reach week 12.

1

Day 0 — Sign up, then land in onboarding

A new user is either coach-invited (in which case onboarding is pre-populated with the coach's inputs) or self-driven. Either way, they hit a 2-step wizard: Set your goal then Add reviewers.

Lowered activation cost (shipped 2026-05-31): onboarding used to ask for up to 10 reviewers before the user had seen any value. Now the bar is one reviewer (recommended, not required), and Claude pre-suggests subgoals from the user's objective text so the cold-start cognitive load drops. Users who finish onboarding with few reviewers see a persistent hub nudge to add more — but only after they've completed their first check-in, so the immediate post-onboarding moment stays clean.
2

Day 1 — The Hub appears

After onboarding completes, the user lands on /individual — the hub. This is the most-visited screen in the entire app. It renders one of four states depending on the user's situation: welcome, caught-up, cycle-complete, or default (pending check-in available).

Q for you: Below the check-in module, the hub has a lot of vertical whitespace. Could it surface "since your last check-in: X" or "this week's pattern is…" in a glanceable way?
3

Week 2 — Insights and Feedback begin to fill

The Insights and Feedback menu items exist from day 1, but they don't have meaningful content until a user has 1–2 weeks of data + stakeholder responses. Until very recently, those pages rendered as visually empty — a first-week user clicked the menu item and thought the app was broken. The improvement plan shipped explicit empty-state messaging for both:

4

Week 6 — The Scorecard becomes the most valuable view

Once enough stakeholder data exists, the Scorecard view (the blind-spot delta between self-rating and reviewer-rating) becomes arguably more valuable than Today's check-in module. This is the page that most differentiates Forbetra from any single-perspective reflection tool.

Q for you: Should the Scorecard become the default hub tab once a user has 4+ weeks of feedback? Today is always default right now. By week 6+ the user's primary motivation has shifted from "let me check in" to "what are people seeing that I'm missing?"
5

Throughout — Progress and Ask

The Progress view is the time-series of effort and performance over the current cycle.

The Ask view is the AI-powered chat against the user's own data. Suggested prompts cover the common questions; the user can also type freely.

6

Long-tail — Stakeholders, History, Journey

Less-visited but high-stakes routes. Stakeholders: manage the up-to-10 reviewer list. History: chronological scrapbook of past weeks. Journey: cycle-by-cycle archive.

7

Week 12 — Cycle complete

End-of-cycle currently is a quiet UI state change — the hub shifts to a "cycle complete" mode and offers a new cycle. For a user who just completed 12 weeks of self-reflection, this is underwhelming.

Q for you: What should the end-of-cycle moment feel like? A celebration page that shows scorecard delta + effort trend + top insight + share-with-coach option would convert a quiet transition into a meaningful moment.

3. The Coach journey — roster, sessions, analytics

Coaches are higher-intent than individuals — they're using the app as their professional tool. They visit 2–4× per week for 5–15 minutes per session. The most important screen is the Session View (the per-client deep dive).

1

Default landing — the Roster

Why "sort by alerts" is the right default: a coach with 10+ clients can't check on each one weekly. The alert sort surfaces who needs attention first — stalled check-ins, missed weeks, big perception gaps.
2

Inviting a new client — the prefilled flow

The coach pre-fills the objective, subgoals, stakeholders, and cycle config. The client signs up via the token link and sees the onboarding wizard pre-populated with the coach's inputs. They just review and confirm.

Q for you: Could a coach save their default objective/subgoal structure as a template? Coaches with a methodology probably repeat the same scaffolding across clients.
3

Session View — the cockpit

The most information-dense screen in the app. Client profile, current objective, subgoals, recent reflections, scores, trend lines, stakeholder feedback summary, AI-generated coach prep, notes (coach-only).

Q for you: A first-time-coach progressive-disclosure tour ("here's the recent activity, here's the AI prep, here's the notes area, dismiss") could reduce overwhelm without dumbing down the page for veterans.

4. The Stakeholder journey — 2 minutes, no signup

This is the only surface for a stakeholder. There's no account, no app history, no return visit. It has to land perfectly because there's no second chance. Mobile-first absolutely required — stakeholders are giving feedback between meetings.

1

The email arrives

Stakeholders get an email every ~2 weeks (Mon-Fri 3pm cron) when the individual completes a check-in. The email contains a tokenized link.

2

The feedback form

The Reveal mechanic: reviewers score blind by default — they don't see the individual's self-score before submitting. This keeps the perception gap honest (no anchoring bias) and gives the individual a trust signal at signup ("reviewers can't see what I rated myself unless I choose to show them"). Individuals can opt-in via /individual/settings if they want reviewers to see their self-scores before submitting.

This is your debugging and observability surface. Not customer-facing; UX bar is lower. The single most useful capability is impersonation — become any user, experience the app as them.

6. Off-stage — what the cron jobs do

The user's experience also includes what arrives in their inbox / on their phone. Seven scheduled jobs (defined in vercel.json) do most of the "I haven't been to the app today" work:

  • remind-base — Mon-Fri 9am. Weekly check-in reminders to individuals.
  • remind-prompts — Mon-Fri 2pm. Overdue check-in nudges (potentially double-tap with 9am — fatigue risk worth watching).
  • remind-feedback — Mon-Fri 3pm. "Time to give feedback" to stakeholders.
  • generate-insights — Sun 8pm. Weekly AI insight generation.
  • coach-prep — Mon 7am. "Your week's coach prep is ready" to coaches.
  • complete-cycles — Daily 1am. End-of-cycle state transitions.
  • stakeholder-impact — 1st of month 10am. "Here's the impact your feedback had" monthly summary to stakeholders.
Q for you: The stakeholder-impact monthly summary is a relationship-builder. Could there be a similar monthly summary for the Individual — "here's what you accomplished this month"? It would tie the cycle together without waiting for the 12-week complete.

7. Friction points worth thinking about — ranked by leverage

Pulled from the journey above. Ordered roughly by impact on first-cycle completion (a leading indicator of long-term engagement).

  1. Empty-state copy on Insights/Feedback/Ask (week 1). Just shipped, but worth A/B testing the wording.
  2. Scorecard as default tab once data exists (~week 4–6). Right now Today is always default; once 4+ weeks of feedback exist, Scorecard is arguably more valuable to land on.
  3. Stakeholder invitation deferral in onboarding. Asking for 10 names + emails before showing value is the biggest drop-off risk. Lower the bar to 1–2 and offer post-first-checkin add-more.
  4. Cycle transition celebration moment (week 12). Quiet state change → underwhelming. A summary moment + share-with-coach option could be a real high.
  5. Suggested subgoals in onboarding step 3. Cold-start is hard. Once the user types an objective in step 2, have Claude suggest 3 subgoals. Big cognitive-load reducer.
  6. First-time Coach session-view tour. Most info-dense screen in the app. A 3-step overlay-then-dismiss could reduce overwhelm.
  7. Reminder cadence + fatigue. Mon-Fri 9am + 2pm could double-tap. Activity-aware scheduling: if checked in within last 2 days, skip the next nudge.
  8. Coach invite templates. Coaches with a methodology repeat subgoals across clients. "Save as template" → reuse.
  9. Mid-cycle objective edit. What if a user realizes their objective is wrong 3 weeks in? Currently no clear "start over" path mid-cycle.
  10. Reveal toggle discoverability. The opt-in/opt-out Reveal is a real trust feature. Make discovery deliberate.
